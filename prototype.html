PCAS1 Prototype

Welcome to the Pedestrian Collision Avoidance System Prototype! The purpose of this prototype is to allow you to gain further understanding on the behavior of this system. To accomplish this, you will be asked a series of questions regarding potential scenarios. Once all the necessary selections have been made, you will be presented with a video demonstration of the system's behavior for the particular set of constraints you selected. When you are done watching the demonstration video for a particular scenario, clicking on the Reset button will return you to the first set of selections, allowing you to investigate a different scenario.

Stationary Pedestrian: (35, 0)

In this scenario, the pedestrian remains stationary at coordinate position x = 35 m, y = 0 m. In order to avoid collision, the vehicle has to slow to a complete stop.

Stationary Pedestrian: (35, -2)

In this scenario, the pedestrian remains stationary at coordinate position x = 35 m, y = -2 m. In this instance, the pedestrian is not moving and is not within the vehicle's collision zone, thus the vehicle is free to continue on at steady state velocity.

Stationary Pedestrian: (35, -4)

In this scenario, the pedestrian remains stationary at coordinate position x = 35 m, y = -4 m. In this instance, the pedestrian is not moving and is not within the vehicle's collision zone, thus the vehicle is free to continue on at steady state velocity.

Moving pedestrian that decides to stop:

In this scenario, the pedestrian begins at position x = 35 m, y = -7 m. They are moving in the positive y directions, perpendicular to the vehicle at a speed of 10 kph. The pedestrian then decideds to stop at position x = 35 m, y = 0 m. To avoid collision, the vehicle must slow to a complete stop.

Moving pedestrian that decides to stop:

In this scenario, the pedestrian begins at position x = 35 m, y = -7 m. They are moving in the positive y directions, perpendicular to the vehicle at a speed of 10 kph. The pedestrian then decideds to stop at position x = 35 m, y = -2 m. To avoid collision, the vehicle begins to slow down after detecting that the pedestrian's trajectory intersects with its path. The pedestrian remains within the collision zone of the vehicle, thus the vehicle comes to a complete stop.

Moving pedestrian that decides to stop:

In this scenario, the pedestrian begins at position x = 35 m, y = -7 m. They are moving in the positive y directions, perpendicular to the vehicle at a speed of 10 kph. The pedestrian then decideds to stop at position x = 35 m, y = -3 m. To avoid collision, the vehicle begins to slow down after detecting that the pedestrian's trajectory intersects with its path. However, once the pedestrian stops, there is no longer a potential for collision, thus the vehicle can return to steady state velocity.

Pedestrian that moves after a time delay:

In this scenario, the pedestrian begins at position x = 35 m, y = 0 m. The pedestrian begins moving after a time delay of 1.5 seconds. To avoid collision, the vehicle slows as it approaches the pedestrian. Once the pedestrian moves out of the vehicle's collision zone, the vehicle can return to steady state velocity and continue on its journey.

Windows executable

If you are running Windows OS 7 or higher and want to investigate this prototype further, follow the steps below to download and run it yourself!

Steps to download and run:

1. Click on this link to download the executable zip file.

2. Unzip the folder. When you unzip you should end up with a folder called. PCAS1_Prototype with an images folder and .exe file inside of it.

3. Double click on the .exe file to run it.

4. If the .exe file is running you should see a new window with a x,y axis, and an image of a car and pedestrian.

5. To run the prototype through scenarios, click on the 'Run' menu bar option and select from the drop-down menu that appears.

6. After one scneario is finished running, the prototype demonstration resets, and you can then select another scenario to run.
